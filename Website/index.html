FAQ

Questions
attorneys
always ask.

If you don't see your question here, your advisor will be able to answer it in your first confidential conversation.

How do I sell my law firm? +
Selling a law firm involves getting a valuation, identifying the right type of buyer, preparing confidential information materials, negotiating deal terms, and planning the client and staff transition. A sell-side advisor manages this process on your behalf so you can focus on running your practice.
What is my law firm worth? +
Most law firm transactions are valued at 0.5x to 1.5x annual revenue, depending on practice area, revenue concentration, growth trajectory, and deal structure. Private equity buyers may pay higher multiples for platform-quality firms with strong systems and scalable revenue.
Is selling to private equity right for me? +
PE-backed deals offer liquidity and growth capital, but typically involve an earn-out period, an equity rollover, and some loss of operational autonomy. They work best for founders who want to stay involved and participate in a second exit — not those looking for a clean break.
Is this process confidential? +
Yes. Your information is never shared without your explicit consent. Advisors you're connected with operate under strict confidentiality — your clients, staff, and opposing counsel won't know you're exploring a sale.
What does it cost to use this platform? +
There is no cost to submit your information or get connected with an advisor. Advisors earn a success fee paid at closing — only if and when a transaction actually occurs. You never pay to explore.
How long does selling a law firm take? +
Most transactions take 6–18 months from initial exploration to closing. Starting early — even if you're not ready to sell for 2–3 years — gives you the most options, the most leverage, and the best final outcome.
